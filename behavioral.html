The STAR Method Framework

S

Situation

Set the context. Describe the situation you were in.

T

Task

Explain the task you had to complete or the goal you were working toward.

A

Action

Describe the specific actions YOU took. Use "I" not "we".

R

Result

Share the outcomes. Quantify when possible.

Story Categories to Prepare

Have 2-3 stories ready for each category. Good stories can span multiple categories.

Leadership

  • • Led a project or initiative
  • • Mentored team members
  • • Made tough decisions
  • • Drove organizational change

Conflict Resolution

  • • Disagreement with teammate
  • • Conflict with manager
  • • Cross-team disagreements
  • • Handled difficult stakeholder

Failure / Mistake

  • • Project that didn't succeed
  • • Mistake you made
  • • What you learned from it
  • • How you improved

Achievement / Impact

  • • Proud accomplishment
  • • Exceeded expectations
  • • Measurable business impact
  • • Recognition received

Innovation

  • • Creative solution
  • • New approach or process
  • • Improved efficiency
  • • Built something new

Tight Deadline / Pressure

  • • Delivered under pressure
  • • Prioritization decisions
  • • Time management
  • • Handled ambiguity

Common Behavioral Questions

Leadership

  • Tell me about a time you led a project from start to finish.
  • Describe a situation where you had to influence without authority.
  • How do you handle underperforming team members?
  • Tell me about a time you mentored someone.

Conflict

  • Tell me about a disagreement with a coworker. How did you resolve it?
  • Describe a time you had to push back on a decision.
  • How do you handle working with difficult personalities?
  • Tell me about a time you received critical feedback.

Challenges

  • What's the most challenging problem you've solved?
  • Tell me about a time you failed. What did you learn?
  • Describe a situation where you had incomplete information.
  • How do you handle ambiguity?

ML-Specific

  • Tell me about an ML project that didn't work. What happened?
  • How do you handle disagreements about technical approaches?
  • Describe a time you had to explain ML concepts to non-technical stakeholders.
  • How do you prioritize which models to build?
